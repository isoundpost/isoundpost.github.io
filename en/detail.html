What is a soundpost in stringed instrument?

In string instrument, the sound post is a cylinder device inside the instrument spanning the space between the top and back plates. The sound post is sometimes referred to as the âme, a French word meaning “soul”. Also, the Italians use the same term, anima, to describe sound post. It serves as a structural support for an archtop instrument, transfers sound from the top plate to the back plate.

What is disadvantage of a traditional sound post?

Mismatch between the sound post and the violin top and back plate, as well as traditional sound post setup methods can cause irreversible damage to the plates. For five hundred years, professional violin makers have been working hard to cut out the sound post of the arc in the resonance box for each violin and find the best sound position. It is a pity that the sound post, which is limited by the quality of the wood, the craftsmanship and the poor fit, can exert an average violin potential of less than 20%, and it will cause serious damage to the violin resonance box.

The overall sound transmission speed of an iSoundpost has exceeded 15,000 m/s, which is much faster than the sound transmission speed of the traditional spruce sound post.
